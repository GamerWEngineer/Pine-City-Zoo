PANDA


Pandas are large, bear-like mammals known for their distinctive black and white fur. They are native to the mountainous regions of central China and primarily feed on bamboo.

Pandas are classified as carnivores, but their diet consists almost entirely of bamboo, which makes up about 99% of their food intake. They have strong jaws and teeth adapted for crushing bamboo stalks.

Pandas are solitary animals, spending most of their time alone except during mating season or when a mother is raising her cubs. They are known for their playful behavior and can often be seen climbing trees or rolling around in the grass.

Pandas are considered a vulnerable species due to habitat loss and low birth rates. Conservation efforts are underway to protect their natural habitats and increase their population in the wild.

Learn more about pandas and how you can help protect them by visiting the World Wildlife Fund.
